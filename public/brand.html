The name derives from a star. The practice derives from Rome.

Alpha Lyrae Design is a naval architecture and product design studio based in Rome. Its work spans yacht design, industrial product design, 3D fabrication, and the project management of new construction.

What distinguishes the studio is a singular proposition: most design offices draw. Yards build. Alpha Lyrae does both. From the first offset table to the final sea trial, one team carries the project.

The studio takes its name from α Lyrae, the star Vega. At 9,602 K, Vega burns blue-white. That precise blue is the brand’s only accent colour — not chosen aesthetically, but measured.

The palette is anchored in the draughting room and the sea. Profondo — deep ocean at night. Avorio — cartridge paper, unworn. Vega — the one colour that is not a choice but a measurement. Mare — the Tyrrhenian in grey weather, the colour of working drawings.

The mark is the name — set precisely, used consistently.

No symbol exists separately from the wordmark. The Lyra constellation functions only as a micro-mark at small scale and as a standalone device in contexts where the full name cannot be reproduced. The constellation’s geometry is astronomical, not decorative.
